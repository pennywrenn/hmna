What this is

But now, we’re all in the kitchen.

One side has been following recipes for a century. Measured, written, repeatable. Codified knowledge passed between the people who knew how things actually worked.

But now the kitchen is open.

The interface is language. And language belongs to everyone. What used to require training now responds to instruction. What used to be written in code can now be spoken.

This is not about replacing the cooks. It’s about expanding who gets to cook. For a long time, a small group wrote the recipes. They controlled the ingredients, the methods, the outcomes.

But now, we’re all in the kitchen. And now, we’re all cooks.
